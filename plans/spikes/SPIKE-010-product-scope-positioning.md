# Objective

Validate whether the product should be positioned as coding-first for MVP or as a general-purpose AI workspace from day one.

# Context

The original goal describes a general-purpose AI workspace, but the architecture and MVP path are coding-heavy through OpenCode, Git, shell, diffs, AGENTS.md, and project files.

# Questions To Answer

 - Who is the first MVP audience?
 - What are the first five workflows the product must support?
 - Which non-code workflows are first-class in MVP, if any?
 - Does coding-first positioning create unacceptable strategic risk?
 - What would validate expansion into writing, research, operations, and documentation?
 - What should be explicitly excluded from MVP messaging?

# Hypothesis

A coding-first MVP is the smallest useful validation path, but product messaging must not overclaim general-purpose support until non-code workflows are first-class.

# Investigation Plan

 - Review existing product scope, MVP docs, and architecture risks.
 - Interview or survey target technical users if available.
 - Compare workflow needs for coding versus document/research users.
 - Define MVP positioning options.
 - Identify claims that would be misleading for MVP.

# Success Criteria

 - MVP audience and positioning are decided.
 - First five MVP workflows are documented.
 - Non-goals are documented.
 - Expansion criteria for general-purpose workflows are defined.

# Decisions Unlocked

 - ADR-001: Product Scope And MVP Audience.
 - ADR-021: UX Layout And Interaction Model.
 - MVP scope and validation plan.

# Estimated Effort

2 to 5 product research days.
